Real-Time Probability & Decision Support Engine

The Problem

High-complexity gaming environments generate continuous data streams but rarely surface actionable analytics to users. Players were forced to make probability-based decisions with incomplete information‚Äîa gap that mirrors clinical decision-making without proper decision support tools.

What I Built

Architected a local-first browser extension that functions as a Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS) prototype. The tool scrapes live DOM data from a closed-source application, structures 408kb+ of unstructured inputs into lookup tables, and calculates real-time risk/reward probabilities to guide user decision-making under uncertainty. The engine processes state across 8 simultaneous players, tracking pool depletion and calculating geometric distribution probabilities with configurable confidence intervals.

Live Interface

Main overlay panel with live pool tracking, target selection, evolution family counting, and probability calculations updating in real-time

Team Tracker: Multi-target tracking with per-roll probability, expected rolls to hit confidence threshold, and combined probability across all targets

Live gameplay view with minimized overlay showing pool state‚Äîexpands on demand for full probability analysis

Configurable polling speeds from 250ms to 5s, balancing update frequency with performance

üìà Probability Engine

  • Hypergeometric/geometric distribution for expected value calculations

  • Configurable confidence intervals (default 75%)

  • LocalStorage persistence for user preferences and settings
